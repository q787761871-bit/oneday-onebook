**Entangled Life:**_**How Fungi Make Our Worlds, Change Our Minds and Shape Our Futures**_ is a 2020 non-fiction book on [mycology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mycology "Mycology") by British [biologist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biologist "Biologist")[Merlin Sheldrake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merlin_Sheldrake "Merlin Sheldrake"). His first book,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-nytimes1-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-3) it was published by [Random House](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Random_House "Random House") on 12 May 2020.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-newyorker2-4) Among the several accolades the book and author have received was the 2021 [Royal Society Prize for Science Books](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Society_Science_Book_Prize "Royal Society Science Book Prize").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-Bayley2021-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-RS2021vid-7)

Sheldrake is an expert in [mycorrhizal fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mycorrhizal_fungi "Mycorrhizal fungi"),[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-newyorker1-8) holds a PhD in tropical ecology from the [University of Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Cambridge "University of Cambridge") for his work on underground fungal networks in tropical forests in Panama, where he was a predoctoral research fellow of the [Smithsonian Tropical Research Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smithsonian_Tropical_Research_Institute "Smithsonian Tropical Research Institute"),[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-observer-9) and he is on the advisory board of the [Society for the Protection of Underground Networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_for_the_Protection_of_Underground_Networks "Society for the Protection of Underground Networks") (SPUN). His research is primarily in the fields of fungal biology and the history of Amazonian [ethnobotany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnobotany "Ethnobotany").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-10) He is the son of [Rupert Sheldrake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rupert_Sheldrake "Rupert Sheldrake"), a New Age author, and [Jill Purce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jill_Purce "Jill Purce"), an author and therapist, and the brother of musician [Cosmo Sheldrake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmo_Sheldrake "Cosmo Sheldrake").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-wsj1-11)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-observer-9)[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-12)

The book looks at [fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi "Fungi") from a number of angles, including [decomposition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decomposition "Decomposition"), [fermentation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermentation "Fermentation"), [nutrient](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nutrient "Nutrient") distribution, [psilocybin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psilocybin "Psilocybin") production, the evolutionary role fungi play in plants, and the ways in which humans relate to the fungal kingdom.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-nytimes1-1)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-wsj1-11)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-newscientist-13)[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-14) It uses music and philosophy to illustrate its thesis,[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-guardian1-15) and introduces readers to a number of central strands of research on mycology.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-16) It is also a personal account of Sheldrake's experiences with fungi.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-newscientist-13)

Jennifer Szalai of _[The New York Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_New\_York\_Times "The New York Times")_ called the book an "ebullient and ambitious exploration" of fungi, adding, "reading it left me not just moved but altered, eager to disseminate its message of what fungi can do."[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-nytimes1-1)[Eugenia Bone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugenia_Bone "Eugenia Bone") of _[The Wall Street Journal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Wall\_Street\_Journal "The Wall Street Journal")_ called it "a gorgeous book of literary [nature writing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nature_writing "Nature writing") in the tradition of [Robert] Macfarlane and John Fowles, ripe with insight and erudition."[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-wsj1-11)[Rachel Cooke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rachel_Cooke "Rachel Cooke") of _[The Observer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Observer "The Observer")_ called it "an astonishing book that could alter our perceptions of fungi forever."[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-observer-9) Richard Kerridge, reviewing the book in _[The Guardian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Guardian "The Guardian")_, wrote that "when we look closely [at fungi], we meet large, unsettling questions... [Sheldrake] carries us easily into these questions with ebullience and precision."[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-guardian1-15)

The book was named on _[Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time\_(magazine) "Time (magazine)")_ magazine's list of the 100 Must-Read Books of 2020[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-17) and _[The Daily Telegraph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Daily\_Telegraph "The Daily Telegraph")_'s list of the 50 Best Books of 2020,[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-18) and was chosen as one of the best nature books of 2020 by _[The Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Times "The Times")_.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-19) It was serialized on [BBC Radio 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Radio_4 "BBC Radio 4") as the book of the week,[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-20) and is a _[Sunday Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunday\_Times "Sunday Times")_ best-seller.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-21) It won the [Wainwright Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wainwright_Prize "Wainwright Prize") in the Global Conservation Writing category,[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-:0-22) the [Guild of Food Writers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guild_of_Food_Writers "Guild of Food Writers") First Book Award,[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-23) and the 2021 [Royal Society Science Book Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Society_Science_Book_Prize "Royal Society Science Book Prize").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-Bayley2021-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-RS2021vid-7) It was shortlisted for the 2021 [British Book Award for Non-Fiction: Narrative Book of the Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Book_Awards#Non-Fiction:_Narrative_Book_of_the_Year "British Book Awards").[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-24)

Sheldrake presented the documentary _Fungi: Web of Life_, narrated by [Björk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk "Björk").[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entangled_Life#cite_note-25)
